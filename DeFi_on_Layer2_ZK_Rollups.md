# DeFi on Layer 2 & ZK Rollups

## What Is DeFi?
Decentralized Finance (DeFi) refers to **financial services on blockchain networks** using smart contracts instead of centralized intermediaries like banks. It includes lending, borrowing, trading, and yield strategies without traditional intermediaries. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

---

## Why Layer 2 Matters for DeFi

Mainnet blockchains like Ethereum often suffer from:
- High gas fees
- Low throughput
- Network congestion

Layer 2 scaling solutions allow DeFi protocols to offer users **cheaper transactions**, faster execution, and improved capital efficiency — crucial for financial apps where cost and speed matter most. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

---

## ZK Rollups Enhancing DeFi

ZK Rollups combine scaling with **validity verification**:
- Transactions are bundled and executed off-chain.
- Zero-knowledge proofs verify correctness without replaying every transaction on mainnet.
- DeFi apps can support high-frequency actions with low cost and high security. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

### Benefits for DeFi Apps
- **Lower fees** – batching reduces per-transaction gas. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}
- **Fast finality** – ZK proofs ensure quick settlement. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}
- **Capital efficiency** – more liquidity flows through markets with fewer delays. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}

## Core Use Cases

### 🔹 Decentralized Exchanges (DEX)
ZK rollups allow fast & cheap swaps, making DEXs more competitive with centralized exchanges.

### 🔹 Lending & Borrowing
Smart contracts can match borrowers and lenders with minimal gas costs.

### 🔹 Yield Strategies & LP Positions
DeFi portfolios can rebalance more economically due to reduced fees.
